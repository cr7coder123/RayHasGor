MODEL HOME-2023 NOVEMBER
The Model House was our main project in the construction course that was worth 70% of our final mark. We were given instructions on how to make the floor with the floor joists and we were also given how to make the walls using the common, trimmer and cripple studs. Our teacher told us that we weren't going to make a roof but I wanted to make it because I was already 1 month ahead so I did. I made the roof so that during the summer the sunlight didn't go in as much and in the winter the sunlight goes in, because I wanted a challenge. I found out how much overhang my rafters should have with a 4-12 pitch by finding out the summer solstice(yellow lines) and the winter solstice(white lines). Finally I cut the birds mouth and placed all my rafters on the house with the same on center as the rest of the house.
MUSKOKA CHAIR-2023 JANUARY
The Muskoka chair was our final 30% in the construction course. The only thing we had was an example of the Muskoka chair. We had to think about how we would construct this by ourselves. I approached the Muskoka chair project by carefully measuring the length and width of each piece. Using the radial arm saw and table saw, I cut the wood planks to precise measurements. Then, I used the planner and jointer to adjust the thickness and ensure a smooth finish. For the challenging curves, I traced the piece and used the band saw, and for the remaining cuts, I used the Mitre saw. After smoothing the corners with the Belt Sander, I assembled the chair. This project tested my skills by using all the machines in the workshop and I was delighted to achieve a 98% on this assignment.
